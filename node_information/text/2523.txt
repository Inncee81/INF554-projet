Le Côté de Guermantes

   after Marcel Proust
   adapted and directed by Christophe Honoré

   Théâtre Marigny
   April June 2020
   Available soon

   © c-album

   Théâtre Marigny

   Le Côté de Guermantes

   2020-04-01 00:00:00 2020-06-30 00:00:00
   Available soon

   For his first production at the Comédie-Française, filmmaker and
   theatre director Christophe Honoré has chosen the third volume of the
   seven that make up « In Search of Lost Time », which Proust began
   writing in 1913.

   The Narrator is a teenager at this point, and has moved to Paris with
   his family, to live in an apartment at the Hôtel de Guermantes, whose
   salon he dreams of attending. The Guermantes own a castle near Combray
   where the boy has already admired the portrait of the Duchess Oriane, a
   figure who becomes the focus of his fascination. Christophe Honoré
   shows that this volume is first and foremost the incarnation of an
   aristocratic family name, inaccessible but suddenly within the
   Narrator’s reach: “Sometimes, hidden deep in her name, the fairy is
   transformed by the shifts of our imagination, through which she lives;
   such was how the atmosphere in which Madame de Guermantes existed
   within me began to lose its colours, after years of being only the
   reflection of a magic lantern and a stained-glass window.” Describing a
   farewell to childhood and the discovery of amorous illusions, this book
   is also a rare account of the bonds of friendship, forged here between
   the Narrator and the Dreyfus supporter Saint-Loup.

   It is at the Théâtre Marigny, a stone’s throw from the gardens where
   the character’s grandmother suffers a dizzy spell that heralds her
   illness, that Christophe Honoré and fifteen actors from the Troupe take
   on the supreme sensitivity of Proust’s language. The director who has
   already brought The Princess of Cleves and The Metamorphoses to the
   screen, who recently staged his own play, Les Idoles and will present
   Tosca at the 2019 Aix-en-Provence International Opera Festival, is
   aware of how literature resists attempts at illustrative adaptations
   and the traps of the commonplace. The extravagance of this work, in his
   view, lies in the sensation of finding something “absolutely familiar”
   to our lives, an ever-pressing shock to the system for the reader,
   which he makes into the vanishing point of his theatrical project.

Comme une pierre qui...

   d'après Greil Marcus
   Adaptation et mise en scène Marie Rémond et Sébastien Pouderoux

   Studio Marigny
   avril mai 2020
   Bientôt disponible

   © c-album

   Studio Marigny

   Comme une pierre qui...

   2020-04-01 00:00:00 2020-05-31 00:00:00
   Bientôt disponible

   Faire sonner le bordel joyeux du rock, tel est le pari relevé par Marie
   Rémond et le sociétaire Sébastien Pouderoux lorsqu’ils créent « Comme
   une pierre qui… » en 2015.

   En 1965, Bob Dylan écrit ce qui deviendra l’une des plus grandes
   chansons rock du XXe siècle, Like a Rolling Stone. Quarante ans plus
   tard, le critique Greil Marcus lui consacre un livre décryptant la
   folle aventure de cette composition : « Like a Rolling Stone était un
   chef-d’œuvre d’habileté, d’inspiration, de volonté et de détermination
   ; mais malgré tous ces éléments, c’était aussi un accident ». À partir
   de cet ouvrage, Marie Rémond et Sébastien Pouderoux s’interrogent sur
   les ressorts de la création – le doute, l’aléatoire, le mystère, la
   poésie – et rendent compte de l’écart entre ce qu’on projette sur la
   naissance d’un chef-d’œuvre et le chaos propre à sa création. On
   découvre ainsi les acteurs de la Troupe en musiciens et chanteurs
   déjantés, couvés par un impresario de légende dont la carrière touche à
   sa fin. En pleine crise identitaire américaine, Dylan semble
   transformer sa rage en interrogation existentielle, « capable de voir
   au cœur des choses, la vérité des choses. Non pas métaphoriquement, en
   fait, mais réellement voir, comme voir dans le métal et le faire fondre
   » écrit l’auteur. Réalise-t-il alors que ces six minutes trente-quatre
   de musique changeront définitivement la face du rock ?

Comme une pierre qui...

   after Greil Marcus
   Adapted and directed by Marie Rémond and Sébastien Pouderoux

   Studio Marigny
   April May 2020
   Available soon

   © c-album

   Studio Marigny

   Comme une pierre qui...

   2020-04-01 00:00:00 2020-05-31 00:00:00
   Available soon

   Letting the “merry mayhem of rock” ring out: this was the challenge
   taken up by Marie Rémond and the sociétaire Sébastien Pouderoux when
   they created "Comme une pierre qui…" [Like a Rolling…] in 2015.

   In 1965, Bob Dylan wrote what would become one of the greatest rock
   songs of the twentieth century, Like a Rolling Stone. Forty years
   later, the critic Greil Marcus devoted a book to it, plunging into the
   crazy adventure of the song’s composition: “Like a Rolling Stone is a
   triumph of craft, inspiration, will and intent; regardless of all those
   things, it was also an accident.” Through this book, Marie Rémond and
   Sébastien Pouderoux ponder the origins of artistic creation – doubt,
   chance, mystery and poetry – and take stock of the difference between
   how we a imagine a masterpiece is born and the chaos that presides over
   its creation. In this production we discover the actors of Troupe as
   off-the-wall musicians and singers pampered by a legendary impresario
   in the twilight of his career. At the height of the American identity
   crisis during the Vietnam War, Dylan seemed to turn his rage into an
   existential questioning, “[he] could see into things, the truth of
   things. Not metaphorically, in fact, but really see, like seeing into
   metal and making it melt” the author writes. Did he realise at the time
   that these six minutes thirty-four seconds of music would change the
   face of rock for all time?

Saint François, le divin jongleur

   de Dario Fo
   Mise en scène Claude Mathieu
   Conception et interprétation Guillaume Gallienne

   Studio
   avril octobre 2020
   Bientôt disponible

   © c-album

   Studio

   Saint François, le divin...

   2020-04-01 00:00:00 2020-10-31 00:00:00
   Bientôt disponible

   Dans l’Italie du XIIIe siècle, où l’insolence conduit rapidement au
   bûcher, François, homme de foi, libre, parcourt l’Ombrie avec ses
   compagnons de pauvreté.

   Sur la route, il restaure les églises, négocie avec le loup, parle aux
   oiseaux et conte en langue vulgaire les Évangiles. Avec joie et malice,
   le saint d’Assise saisit les badauds, les amuse, les émeut tout en
   dénonçant l’argent, le pouvoir, la manipulation et la guerre. François
   « harangue la foule, il parle avec tout son corps et toute sa voix.
   L’écouter, c’est assister à un spectacle, à une comédie religieuse. Il
   a le savoir-faire d’un grand acteur et d’un saint jongleur ». Pour
   poursuivre ses prédications avec ses frères les pauvres, saint François
   doit convaincre le pape Innocent des bienfaits de sa communauté.

   « Pour moi, le solo c’est une liberté totale. Je m’inspire beaucoup de
   la démarche de Dario Fo, proche du théâtre de rue. Le solo me semble
   aussi aller vers ce dépouillement, puisque subsiste ici la parole
   seule. Débarrassé de la notion de dialogue, ce théâtre renoue avec le
   conte et établit une relation plus directe avec les spectateurs. Ici,
   je ne suis pas un acteur, je suis un conteur qui véhicule la parole
   d’un auteur, qui livre un récit au public en le sollicitant constamment
   » dit Guillaume Gallienne que Claude Mathieu, sa marraine de théâtre et
   doyenne de la Troupe, met en scène : « Un texte, un comédien de noir
   vêtu, une table, un tabouret et un projecteur poursuite, une formidable
   envie de fouiller, de trouver ce “Tout” à partir de “Rien”, tout cela
   si proche de notre saint François, père de la pauvreté dans son
   itinérance qui devenait doucement la nôtre… »

Saint François, le divin jongleur

   by Dario Fo
   directed by Claude Mathieu
   performed by Guillaume Gallienne

   Studio
   April October 2020
   Available soon

   © c-album

   Studio

   Saint François, le divin...

   2020-04-01 00:00:00 2020-10-31 00:00:00
   Available soon

   In thirteenth-century Italy, where insolence quickly led to the stake,
   Francis, a free man, a man of faith, wandered through Umbria with his
   companions in poverty.

   Along the way, he restored churches, negotiated with the wolf, spoke to
   the birds and preached the Gospels in the common language. With joy and
   malice, the Saint of Assisi captured the attention of onlookers, amused
   them and moved them while denouncing money, power, manipulation and
   war. Francis “harangues the crowd, he speaks with his entire body and
   the fullness of his voice. To listen to him is to attend a show, a
   religious comedy. He has the know-how of a great actor and a holy
   jester.” To continue his preaching with his poor brothers, St. Francis
   must convince Pope Innocent of the benefits of his community.

   “For me, a solo means total freedom. I am very inspired by the approach
   of Dario Fo, which is close to street theatre. I think the solo also
   leads to a stripping back of the performance, since all that remains is
   the spoken word. In freeing itself from the notion of dialogue, this
   theatre goes back to storytelling and establishes a more direct
   relationship with the audience. Here, I am not an actor, I am a
   storyteller who conveys the words of an author, who presents a story to
   the audience while constantly addressing it” comments Guillaume
   Gallienne, whom Claude Mathieu, his theatre mentor and the Troupe
   doyenne, is directing: “A text, an actor dressed in black, a table, a
   stool and a followspot, a formidable desire to probe, to find this
   “Everything” from “Nothing”, all this so close to our Saint Francis,
   the father of poverty, whose itinerancy slowly became ours...”

Le Côté de Guermantes

   after Marcel Proust
   adapted and directed by Christophe Honoré

   Théâtre Marigny
   April June 2020
   Available soon

   © c-album

   Théâtre Marigny

   Le Côté de Guermantes

   2020-04-01 00:00:00 2020-06-30 00:00:00
   Available soon

   For his first production at the Comédie-Française, filmmaker and
   theatre director Christophe Honoré has chosen the third volume of the
   seven that make up « In Search of Lost Time », which Proust began
   writing in 1913.

   The Narrator is a teenager at this point, and has moved to Paris with
   his family, to live in an apartment at the Hôtel de Guermantes, whose
   salon he dreams of attending. The Guermantes own a castle near Combray
   where the boy has already admired the portrait of the Duchess Oriane, a
   figure who becomes the focus of his fascination. Christophe Honoré
   shows that this volume is first and foremost the incarnation of an
   aristocratic family name, inaccessible but suddenly within the
   Narrator’s reach: “Sometimes, hidden deep in her name, the fairy is
   transformed by the shifts of our imagination, through which she lives;
   such was how the atmosphere in which Madame de Guermantes existed
   within me began to lose its colours, after years of being only the
   reflection of a magic lantern and a stained-glass window.” Describing a
   farewell to childhood and the discovery of amorous illusions, this book
   is also a rare account of the bonds of friendship, forged here between
   the Narrator and the Dreyfus supporter Saint-Loup.

   It is at the Théâtre Marigny, a stone’s throw from the gardens where
   the character’s grandmother suffers a dizzy spell that heralds her
   illness, that Christophe Honoré and fifteen actors from the Troupe take
   on the supreme sensitivity of Proust’s language. The director who has
   already brought The Princess of Cleves and The Metamorphoses to the
   screen, who recently staged his own play, Les Idoles and will present
   Tosca at the 2019 Aix-en-Provence International Opera Festival, is
   aware of how literature resists attempts at illustrative adaptations
   and the traps of the commonplace. The extravagance of this work, in his
   view, lies in the sensation of finding something “absolutely familiar”
   to our lives, an ever-pressing shock to the system for the reader,
   which he makes into the vanishing point of his theatrical project.

Comme une pierre qui...

   d'après Greil Marcus
   Adaptation et mise en scène Marie Rémond et Sébastien Pouderoux

   Studio Marigny
   avril mai 2020
   Bientôt disponible

   © c-album

   Studio Marigny

   Comme une pierre qui...

   2020-04-01 00:00:00 2020-05-31 00:00:00
   Bientôt disponible

   Faire sonner le bordel joyeux du rock, tel est le pari relevé par Marie
   Rémond et le sociétaire Sébastien Pouderoux lorsqu’ils créent « Comme
   une pierre qui… » en 2015.

   En 1965, Bob Dylan écrit ce qui deviendra l’une des plus grandes
   chansons rock du XXe siècle, Like a Rolling Stone. Quarante ans plus
   tard, le critique Greil Marcus lui consacre un livre décryptant la
   folle aventure de cette composition : « Like a Rolling Stone était un
   chef-d’œuvre d’habileté, d’inspiration, de volonté et de détermination
   ; mais malgré tous ces éléments, c’était aussi un accident ». À partir
   de cet ouvrage, Marie Rémond et Sébastien Pouderoux s’interrogent sur
   les ressorts de la création – le doute, l’aléatoire, le mystère, la
   poésie – et rendent compte de l’écart entre ce qu’on projette sur la
   naissance d’un chef-d’œuvre et le chaos propre à sa création. On
   découvre ainsi les acteurs de la Troupe en musiciens et chanteurs
   déjantés, couvés par un impresario de légende dont la carrière touche à
   sa fin. En pleine crise identitaire américaine, Dylan semble
   transformer sa rage en interrogation existentielle, « capable de voir
   au cœur des choses, la vérité des choses. Non pas métaphoriquement, en
   fait, mais réellement voir, comme voir dans le métal et le faire fondre
   » écrit l’auteur. Réalise-t-il alors que ces six minutes trente-quatre
   de musique changeront définitivement la face du rock ?

Comme une pierre qui...

   after Greil Marcus
   Adapted and directed by Marie Rémond and Sébastien Pouderoux

   Studio Marigny
   April May 2020
   Available soon

   © c-album

   Studio Marigny

   Comme une pierre qui...

   2020-04-01 00:00:00 2020-05-31 00:00:00
   Available soon

   Letting the “merry mayhem of rock” ring out: this was the challenge
   taken up by Marie Rémond and the sociétaire Sébastien Pouderoux when
   they created "Comme une pierre qui…" [Like a Rolling…] in 2015.

   In 1965, Bob Dylan wrote what would become one of the greatest rock
   songs of the twentieth century, Like a Rolling Stone. Forty years
   later, the critic Greil Marcus devoted a book to it, plunging into the
   crazy adventure of the song’s composition: “Like a Rolling Stone is a
   triumph of craft, inspiration, will and intent; regardless of all those
   things, it was also an accident.” Through this book, Marie Rémond and
   Sébastien Pouderoux ponder the origins of artistic creation – doubt,
   chance, mystery and poetry – and take stock of the difference between
   how we a imagine a masterpiece is born and the chaos that presides over
   its creation. In this production we discover the actors of Troupe as
   off-the-wall musicians and singers pampered by a legendary impresario
   in the twilight of his career. At the height of the American identity
   crisis during the Vietnam War, Dylan seemed to turn his rage into an
   existential questioning, “[he] could see into things, the truth of
   things. Not metaphorically, in fact, but really see, like seeing into
   metal and making it melt” the author writes. Did he realise at the time
   that these six minutes thirty-four seconds of music would change the
   face of rock for all time?

Saint François, le divin jongleur

   de Dario Fo
   Mise en scène Claude Mathieu
   Conception et interprétation Guillaume Gallienne

   Studio
   avril octobre 2020
   Bientôt disponible

   © c-album

   Studio

   Saint François, le divin...

   2020-04-01 00:00:00 2020-10-31 00:00:00
   Bientôt disponible

   Dans l’Italie du XIIIe siècle, où l’insolence conduit rapidement au
   bûcher, François, homme de foi, libre, parcourt l’Ombrie avec ses
   compagnons de pauvreté.

   Sur la route, il restaure les églises, négocie avec le loup, parle aux
   oiseaux et conte en langue vulgaire les Évangiles. Avec joie et malice,
   le saint d’Assise saisit les badauds, les amuse, les émeut tout en
   dénonçant l’argent, le pouvoir, la manipulation et la guerre. François
   « harangue la foule, il parle avec tout son corps et toute sa voix.
   L’écouter, c’est assister à un spectacle, à une comédie religieuse. Il
   a le savoir-faire d’un grand acteur et d’un saint jongleur ». Pour
   poursuivre ses prédications avec ses frères les pauvres, saint François
   doit convaincre le pape Innocent des bienfaits de sa communauté.

   « Pour moi, le solo c’est une liberté totale. Je m’inspire beaucoup de
   la démarche de Dario Fo, proche du théâtre de rue. Le solo me semble
   aussi aller vers ce dépouillement, puisque subsiste ici la parole
   seule. Débarrassé de la notion de dialogue, ce théâtre renoue avec le
   conte et établit une relation plus directe avec les spectateurs. Ici,
   je ne suis pas un acteur, je suis un conteur qui véhicule la parole
   d’un auteur, qui livre un récit au public en le sollicitant constamment
   » dit Guillaume Gallienne que Claude Mathieu, sa marraine de théâtre et
   doyenne de la Troupe, met en scène : « Un texte, un comédien de noir
   vêtu, une table, un tabouret et un projecteur poursuite, une formidable
   envie de fouiller, de trouver ce “Tout” à partir de “Rien”, tout cela
   si proche de notre saint François, père de la pauvreté dans son
   itinérance qui devenait doucement la nôtre… »

Saint François, le divin jongleur

   by Dario Fo
   directed by Claude Mathieu
   performed by Guillaume Gallienne

   Studio
   April October 2020
   Available soon

   © c-album

   Studio

   Saint François, le divin...

   2020-04-01 00:00:00 2020-10-31 00:00:00
   Available soon

   In thirteenth-century Italy, where insolence quickly led to the stake,
   Francis, a free man, a man of faith, wandered through Umbria with his
   companions in poverty.

   Along the way, he restored churches, negotiated with the wolf, spoke to
   the birds and preached the Gospels in the common language. With joy and
   malice, the Saint of Assisi captured the attention of onlookers, amused
   them and moved them while denouncing money, power, manipulation and
   war. Francis “harangues the crowd, he speaks with his entire body and
   the fullness of his voice. To listen to him is to attend a show, a
   religious comedy. He has the know-how of a great actor and a holy
   jester.” To continue his preaching with his poor brothers, St. Francis
   must convince Pope Innocent of the benefits of his community.

   “For me, a solo means total freedom. I am very inspired by the approach
   of Dario Fo, which is close to street theatre. I think the solo also
   leads to a stripping back of the performance, since all that remains is
   the spoken word. In freeing itself from the notion of dialogue, this
   theatre goes back to storytelling and establishes a more direct
   relationship with the audience. Here, I am not an actor, I am a
   storyteller who conveys the words of an author, who presents a story to
   the audience while constantly addressing it” comments Guillaume
   Gallienne, whom Claude Mathieu, his theatre mentor and the Troupe
   doyenne, is directing: “A text, an actor dressed in black, a table, a
   stool and a followspot, a formidable desire to probe, to find this
   “Everything” from “Nothing”, all this so close to our Saint Francis,
   the father of poverty, whose itinerancy slowly became ours...”
