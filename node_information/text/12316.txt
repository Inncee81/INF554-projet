Discours de Jacques Chirac lors de l'inauguration du mahJ en 1998

   Jacques Chirac, dont nous apprenons le décès, a joué un rôle majeur
   dans la création du musée d’art et d’histoire du Judaïsme (mahJ). En
   effet, on lui doit, dans les années 1980, la décision de mettre l’hôtel
   de Saint-Aignan, dans le Marais, à la disposition du futur musée. Un
   accord original entre Jack Lang, alors ministre de la Culture, et
   Jacques Chirac, alors maire de Paris, fut signé dans ce sens en 1986.
   Il permit la création d’une association de préfiguration en 1988 et
   l’ouverture du mahJ en 1998, sous la direction de Laurence Sigal. Le
   mahJ, « musée de France », est soutenu depuis de manière paritaire par
   l’État et la Ville.

   Jacques Chirac inaugura le mahJ (voir la vidéo) en tant que président
   de la République, le 30 novembre 1998, aux côtés de Catherine
   Trautmann, ministre de la Culture et de la Communication, et de
   Claude-Gérard Marcus, président du musée et inspirateur du projet.

Discover the permanent collection

   With seven hundred artworks and objects, the permanent collection
   proposes a history of the Jewish communities of Europe and the
   Mediterranean from the Middle Ages to today.

   Attention : Please note that the museum will be closed on October 1^st
   (Rosh ha-Shanah) as well as on October 9^th (Yom Kippur).

   The mahJ is open from Tuesday to Sunday and closed on Monday (except
   for group visits with reservations).

   For your safety, there are heightened security procedures when you
   arrive at the museum.

   Opening hours till October 16^th, 2019
   Tuesday to Friday: 11 am–6 pm
   Saturday and Sunday: 10 am–6 pm
   Last ticket sales 45 minutes before closure.

   Closed every Monday.
   Closed on October 1^st (Rosh ha-Shana), and October 9^th (Kippur).

Adolfo Kaminsky. Forger and photographer

   From Thursday 23 May 2019 until Sunday 8 December 2019

   Adolfo Kaminsky, a member of the Resistance and a brilliant forger,
   spent thirty years of his life producing counterfeit identity papers to
   save lives. He discovered photography during the Second World War
   reproducing official stamps for forged identity cards.

Discours de Jacques Chirac lors de l'inauguration du mahJ en 1998

   Jacques Chirac, dont nous apprenons le décès, a joué un rôle majeur
   dans la création du musée d’art et d’histoire du Judaïsme (mahJ). En
   effet, on lui doit, dans les années 1980, la décision de mettre l’hôtel
   de Saint-Aignan, dans le Marais, à la disposition du futur musée. Un
   accord original entre Jack Lang, alors ministre de la Culture, et
   Jacques Chirac, alors maire de Paris, fut signé dans ce sens en 1986.
   Il permit la création d’une association de préfiguration en 1988 et
   l’ouverture du mahJ en 1998, sous la direction de Laurence Sigal. Le
   mahJ, « musée de France », est soutenu depuis de manière paritaire par
   l’État et la Ville.

   Jacques Chirac inaugura le mahJ (voir la vidéo) en tant que président
   de la République, le 30 novembre 1998, aux côtés de Catherine
   Trautmann, ministre de la Culture et de la Communication, et de
   Claude-Gérard Marcus, président du musée et inspirateur du projet.

Discover the permanent collection

   With seven hundred artworks and objects, the permanent collection
   proposes a history of the Jewish communities of Europe and the
   Mediterranean from the Middle Ages to today.

   Attention : Please note that the museum will be closed on October 1^st
   (Rosh ha-Shanah) as well as on October 9^th (Yom Kippur).

   The mahJ is open from Tuesday to Sunday and closed on Monday (except
   for group visits with reservations).

   For your safety, there are heightened security procedures when you
   arrive at the museum.

   Opening hours till October 16^th, 2019
   Tuesday to Friday: 11 am–6 pm
   Saturday and Sunday: 10 am–6 pm
   Last ticket sales 45 minutes before closure.

   Closed every Monday.
   Closed on October 1^st (Rosh ha-Shana), and October 9^th (Kippur).

Adolfo Kaminsky. Forger and photographer

   From Thursday 23 May 2019 until Sunday 8 December 2019

   Adolfo Kaminsky, a member of the Resistance and a brilliant forger,
   spent thirty years of his life producing counterfeit identity papers to
   save lives. He discovered photography during the Second World War
   reproducing official stamps for forged identity cards.

   La mallette des familles

Come and discover the mahJ's collections with your family

   and enjoy the family kit. It's booklet, full of activities designed for
   playing and creating, is also in English. The family kit is available
   free of charge at the ticket desk.

   Good to know : some of these are particularly adapted to children from
   three years of age.
   Walking tour of the Jewish Marais

Guided tours available for groups

   The guided tours "Discover the mahJ" and the "Walking tour of the
   Jewish district of the Marais, in Paris" are available in English,
   German, Hebrew, and Spanish.

   These guided tours are also planned every Sunday from mid-May to
   mid-August at 3 pm.
