The occultation of dwarf planet Makemake on 23 April 2011

   This animation shows the path of the shadow of the dwarf planet
   Makemake during an occultation of a faint star in April 2011. Several
   sites in South America saw the star briefly disappear as its light was
   blocked by Makemake. This dwarf planet is about two thirds of the size
   of Pluto, and travels around the Sun in a distant path that lies beyond
   that of Pluto, but closer to the Sun than Eris, the most massive known
   dwarf planet in the Solar System. Makemake was expected to have an
   atmosphere like Pluto, but the new occultation data shows that this is
   not the case. Note: the actual shape of the shadow on Earth will not be
   exactly round as shown here. This video is to illustrate the
   phenomenon.

The occultation of dwarf planet Makemake on 23 April 2011

   This animation shows the path of the shadow of the dwarf planet
   Makemake during an occultation of a faint star in April 2011. Several
   sites in South America saw the star briefly disappear as its light was
   blocked by Makemake. This dwarf planet is about two thirds of the size
   of Pluto, and travels around the Sun in a distant path that lies beyond
   that of Pluto, but closer to the Sun than Eris, the most massive known
   dwarf planet in the Solar System. Makemake was expected to have an
   atmosphere like Pluto, but the new occultation data shows that this is
   not the case. Note: the actual shape of the shadow on Earth will not be
   exactly round as shown here. This video is to illustrate the
   phenomenon.

The occultation of dwarf planet Makemake on 23 April 2011

   This animation shows the path of the shadow of the dwarf planet
   Makemake during an occultation of a faint star in April 2011. Several
   sites in South America saw the star briefly disappear as its light was
   blocked by Makemake. This dwarf planet is about two thirds of the size
   of Pluto, and travels around the Sun in a distant path that lies beyond
   that of Pluto, but closer to the Sun than Eris, the most massive known
   dwarf planet in the Solar System. Makemake was expected to have an
   atmosphere like Pluto, but the new occultation data shows that this is
   not the case. Note: the actual shape of the shadow on Earth will not be
   exactly round as shown here. This video is to illustrate the
   phenomenon.

Zákryt hvězdy trpasličí planetou Makemake 23. dubna 2011

   Animace ukazuje vzájemný pohyb Makemake a slabé hvězdy při zákrytu v
   dubnu 2011. Úkaz (zmizení hvězdy zastíněné Makemake) byl pozorován na
   několika místech jižní Ameriky včetně observatoří La Silla a Paranal.
   Makemake je těleso asi o třetinu menší než Pluto. Kolem Slunce obíhá
   pro dráze za oběžnou drahou Pluta, ale blíže než Eris, nejhmotnější
   známá trpasličí planeta ve Sluneční soustavě. Předpokládalo se, že
   Makemake by mohla mít podobnou atmosféru, jakou má Pluto. Poslední
   pozorování však ukazují, že Makamake atmosféru nemá.

Okkultationen af dværgplaneten Makemake den 23. april 2011

   Denne animation viser bevægelsen af dværgplaneten Makemakes skygge
   under okkultationen af en svag stjerne i april 2011. Flere steder i
   Sydamerika så kortvarigt den svage stjerne forsvinde, mens dens lys
   blev blokeret af Makemake. Denne dværgplanet har en størrelse på
   omkring to tredjedele af Plutos og den kredser om Solen i en fjern
   bane, der ligger længere væk end Plutos, men tættere på Solen end Eris,
   der er den tungeste dværgplanet i Solsystemet. Det var forventet, at
   Makemake har en atmosfære ligesom Pluto, men det har nu vist sig ikke
   at være tilfældet.
