A EBG Antenna comprising a lateral wall surrounding an axis

Référence

   84424-02

Mots-clés

   Antenna, Photonic forbiddent band (pfb), Photonic band gap (pbg), Wide
   band, Microwave, Reflective plane

Statut des brevets

   French priority patent application FR0405485 filed on May 19th, 2004

Inventeurs

   Bernard JECKO
   Laure FREYTAG
   Elisa POINTEREAU

Statut commercial

   Exclusive or non-exclusive licence Collaborative research

Laboratoire

   XLIM (UMR7252), Limoges, France

Description

CONTEXT

   The invention seeks to improve the gain antennas of EBG material with a
   side wall surrounding an axis.
   The introduction of a break (or defect) into the geometric periodicity
   of the EBG material makes it possible to produce an absorption defect
   and hence to create a narrow passband within a wide stopband.
   The EBG material antennas have the advantage of having a very good
   directivity with reduced dimensions (2D) compared to dish, lens or
   horn-type antennas. The EBG antenna allows multifunctional modes
   (multiband and multipolar).

TECHNICAL DESCRIPTION

   The invention seeks to improve the gain antennas of EBG material with a
   side wall surrounding an axis.
   A antenna made from a EBG (Electromagnetic BandGap) material, includes:
   a lateral wall (4) which is made from an EBG material and which
   completely surrounds a central axis (6), the wall being disposed at a
   distance from the central axis such as to form a central resonant
   cavity; and at least one radiating element (34) which is placed inside
   the cavity.
   Each radiating element is positioned inside the cavity in order to
   excite an electromagnetic field parallel to the central axis and the
   radiating element(s) can excite the modes of the central cavity having
   a radial resonance more strongly than the other modes of the cavity.

BENEFITS

     * Omnidirectional
     * Very good gain (about 8 times better than similar antennas with
       dipole for example)
     * Possible use of metal EBG material (less expensive than a
       dielectric EBG material)

INDUSTRIAL APPLICATIONS

   Antenna useful for base station in mobile telephony
